DRC Market Structure

Why Western buyers struggle to source DRC cobalt directly

DRC produces ~73% of global mined cobalt, but China refines ~78% of it. The supply chain has two chokepoints: Chinese refiners downstream, and Chinese-backed traders (CMOC, Sicomines) and state intermediaries (Gécamines via Mercuria, EGC mandatory offtake) upstream. A Western buyer who calls a DRC mine directly finds either a Chinese-controlled operation or a state entity routing through a state-approved intermediary. Independent Western-credentialled producers exist — they are the structural opportunity this business is built on.

Key vocabulary — what terms mean in practice

TermWhat it means for your deal
Payable% of contained metal you actually pay for. 97% on cobalt hydroxide is standard. 95% = worse deal. Negotiate this first.
FOB / CIF / DDPFOB: you take risk at port of loading. CIF: seller pays insurance and freight to named port. DDP: seller delivers to your door, all costs paid. Price increases FOB → CIF → DDP.
Hydroxide vs metal vs sulphateHydroxide is the DRC intermediate. Metal is refined. Sulphate is battery-ready. Confirm which your process requires before any RFQ — specs and pricing differ significantly.
Offtake agreementForward commitment to buy a specified volume at an agreed pricing formula. Creates supply certainty for buyer, revenue certainty for seller. The document you want before you reveal counterparty identities.
RMI complianceResponsible Minerals Initiative audit. Required by most Western EV, aerospace and defence buyers. Non-compliant supplier cannot close regardless of price.
ARECOMS quotaDRC government annual export cap. Currently 87,000t cobalt/yr through 2027. New strategic reserve means state can withhold unused quota from market.
TC/RCTreatment charge / refining charge. Fee smelters charge to process concentrate. Affects economics of toll-refining arrangements.
Black massShredded EV battery material before hydromet processing. Growing recycled cobalt feedstock. Affects primary pricing as recycled supply grows.
Warrant holderLME warrant = ownership of a specific lot in an LME warehouse. Warrant positions reveal physical tightness not visible in futures prices.
MHPMixed hydroxide precipitate. Intermediate nickel-cobalt product. Increasingly relevant as Western refiners build direct MHP-to-battery chemical routes.
Current market context — April 2026

The structural shift happening right now

Three things are happening simultaneously that have not aligned before: DRC is centralising supply control (strategic reserve + Gécamines-Mercuria JV + Glencore security framework). Western governments are building alternative procurement infrastructure (Section 232 tariffs, EU REsourceEU, US-EU coordination pact). And the Copperbelt export infrastructure just broke (Lobito). The result: buyers who have not built direct DRC relationships are about to discover that their broker, their trading house contact, and their existing offtake are all intermediated through the same two or three entities — and those entities are under state direction. The buyers who act in Q2 2026 will have materially different supply chain options in 2027 than those who wait.

The opportunity for independent suppliers right now

The Gécamines-Mercuria 500,000t commitment and the DRC strategic reserve have, paradoxically, strengthened the pitch of independent Western-credentialled producers. Every tonne that flows through Mercuria is a tonne that Western buyers cannot claim as a direct, transparent, RMI-verified supply chain. The regulatory pressure on Western manufacturers — EU Battery Regulation, US IRA domestic content requirements, defence supply chain traceability — means "bought through a trader" is becoming an inadequate answer to a CFO or regulator. Independent producers who can demonstrate Western-credentialled, RMI-compliant direct offtake are not competing with Gécamines — they are in a different market segment that Gécamines cannot serve.
